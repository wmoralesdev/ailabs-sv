## Brand Personality

If AI Labs SV were a person, they'd be the friend who stays up late asking "but what if we tried it this way?" — someone who's deeply knowledgeable but never talks down to you, who celebrates your half-finished experiments as much as your wins, and who'd rather ask a better question than give a polished answer.

Bold, direct, curious, inclusive, grounded. Never corporate. Never performative. Always building.

## Voice Attributes

### Bold

- **We are:** Confident, declarative, willing to take a stance. We say things directly and don't hedge.
- **We are not:** Aggressive, arrogant, or dismissive of other perspectives.
- **This sounds like:** "48 hours to answer one question: what can we build?"
- **This does NOT sound like:** "We invite you to consider participating in our upcoming event."

### Direct

- **We are:** Clear, concise, anti-jargon. We get to the point. No filler, no fluff.
- **We are not:** Blunt to the point of being cold, or so terse we lose warmth.
- **This sounds like:** "No slides. Just real experiments."
- **This does NOT sound like:** "We aim to create an environment focused on practical outcomes."

### Curious

- **We are:** Question-driven, exploratory, genuinely interested. We lead with "why" and "what if."
- **We are not:** Aimless, unfocused, or intellectually performative. Curiosity is our engine, not our aesthetic.
- **This sounds like:** "Got questions? Good."
- **This does NOT sound like:** "Leveraging best practices."

### Inclusive

- **We are:** Open, welcoming, anti-gatekeeping. The only entry requirement is genuine curiosity. Credentials are welcome — they're just not required.
- **We are not:** Lowering standards or being vague about what we value. We're inclusive about *who*, not about *effort*.
- **This sounds like:** "No gatekeeping. Just curious people."
- **This does NOT sound like:** "Apply to be considered for membership."

### Grounded

- **We are:** Honest about where we are, rooted in El Salvador, specific rather than grandiose.
- **We are not:** Self-deprecating or small-thinking. Grounded doesn't mean modest about our ambition.
- **This sounds like:** "A community of builders in El Salvador."
- **This does NOT sound like:** "A world-class innovation ecosystem."

## Audience Awareness

### Primary: Builders in El Salvador

Developers, designers, and technical people already working in tech who want to explore AI tools. They're hands-on, they ship, they're looking for peers — not teachers. They know enough to be dangerous and want to be around others who are building.

### Secondary: Curious leaders and non-technical technologists

CTOs, product people, and team leads who want to understand AI through proximity to builders. They attend to absorb and ask questions, not just to present or network.

### Tertiary: Partners, speakers, educators, and sponsors

Companies, individuals, and academic institutions who want to engage with this community — to recruit from, learn alongside, teach within, or align their brand with curiosity-driven building.

### How we speak to all three

We treat everyone the same. No code-switching between audiences. The language is always clear enough for the curious newcomer and substantive enough for the experienced builder. We don't explain things "for beginners" — we explain things clearly, period.

## Core Messaging Pillars

In priority order, these are the themes that run through everything we say:

### 1. Curiosity is the engine

Everything starts with a question. Our community exists because someone asked "what if?" and kept going. Curiosity isn't a nice-to-have trait — it's the core qualification. "Curiosity over credentials" is the operating principle. That means credentials are never required to participate — but they're always welcome when someone wants to share what they know.

### 2. Experimentation first

We lead with building. Our default mode is hands-on: ask a question, build something to answer it, share what you learn. That's the heartbeat of AI Labs SV. But "experimentation first" is a priority, not a rule. A structured workshop, a guest lecture from a university partner, a presentation that teaches something real — all of that has a place when it serves the community. The filter isn't the format. The filter is: did people leave knowing more than when they arrived?

### 3. Accessible by default

No gatekeeping. No credentialism. The barrier to entry is genuine curiosity, nothing more. We keep barriers low — whether that means free events, affordable tickets, or flexible formats. Accessibility is about ensuring that who you are, where you studied, or what title you hold never determines whether you can participate. Some events are free. Some carry a cost to sustain the experience. The commitment to accessibility doesn't change either way.

### 4. Questions over answers

The point isn't to have answers — it's to keep asking better questions. We measure success by the quality of questions explored, not by the polish of the output. "You'll leave with better questions than you came with" is our brand promise.

### 5. AI is the tool, not the identity

AI is the sharpest, highest-leverage tool for curious technologists right now. If ChatGPT disappeared tomorrow, nothing changes — the community explores with whatever tools exist. Curiosity survives any product cycle.

## Tone Spectrum

The voice stays the same. The tone dials up or down depending on context.

### By channel

| Channel | Tone | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| Website / landing page | Bold, declarative, manifesto-like | "Curiosity is what drives us. AI is how we explore." |
| Social media | Punchy, direct, question-led | "What would you build if you had 48 hours and one question?" |
| Event descriptions | Action-oriented, inviting, specific | "Bring your laptop and your questions." |
| Blog / learning content | Thoughtful, exploratory, honest about failure | "Three experiments that failed, and why they matter more than the ones that worked." |
| Email / newsletter | Personal, conversational, sharing-focused | "Here's what we've been learning this month." |
| Partner communications | Confident, specific, mission-anchored | "We're a community of 500+ builders. Here's how we work." |

### By situation

| Situation | Tone shift |
|-----------|-----------|
| Event launch | Dial up boldness and energy. Lead with the question the event will explore. |
| Community milestone | Celebratory but grounded. Credit the community, not the organization. |
| Sharing failures or learnings | Honest and curious. Failures are content, not embarrassments. |
| Recruiting partners or sponsors | Confident but never salesy. Lead with the community's value, not metrics. |
| Onboarding new members | Warm and clear. Make the first step obvious and low-pressure. |
| Paid event communication | Transparent about the cost and what it enables. The value should be self-evident. Never apologize for charging — explain what the ticket makes possible. |
| Incident or mistake | Transparent and direct. Own it, explain it, move forward. |

### The rule

Bold and warm always coexist. In energetic moments, dial up bold. In sensitive moments, dial up warm. Neither attribute ever disappears.

## Style Rules

### Grammar and mechanics

| Rule | Our choice | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Oxford comma | Yes | "ask questions, run experiments, and share what they learn" |
| Headings | Sentence case | "Where questions become projects" |
| Contractions | Use them | "we're," "don't," "it's" — they keep the tone human |
| Em dashes | No spaces | "starting in El Salvador — growing across Latin America" |
| Numbers | Numerals for stats, spell out in prose | "500+ builders" but "one question" |
| Percent | Symbol | "40%" |
| Exclamation marks | Sparingly — max one per page or section | Never more than one. Overuse kills the bold tone. |
| ALL CAPS | Only for section badges/labels | "THE LAB," "THE STORY," "WHY" — never in body copy |
| Emojis | Avoid in primary copy | Acceptable in social media and community channels, never on the website |
| Ellipsis | Avoid | Trails off instead of committing. We commit. |

### Sentence structure

Short sentences. Declarative. When we use fragments, they're intentional — punchy, not lazy.

We favor the pattern: statement, then proof. "We believe the best way to understand AI is to ask your own questions. Not follow a curriculum. Not watch a tutorial."

We break convention when it serves clarity: "No slides. Just real experiments." is better than "Instead of presentations, we showcase real experiments."

### Formatting

- **Bold** for emphasis, used sparingly
- *Italics* for titles or introducing terms
- Link text is always descriptive — never "click here"
- Lists only when scanning matters (event details, resources). Prose for everything else.

## Terminology

### Words we use

| Term | Why |
|------|-----|
| Builders | Our people. Not "members," "users," or "attendees." |
| Curious | The only credential that matters. |
| Explore / exploring | Our primary verb. Not "leverage," "utilize," or "implement." |
| Experiments | What we run. Not "projects" when we mean work-in-progress. |
| Questions | What we start with. Always. |
| Share | What we do with what we learn. Not "ship" as the primary verb. |
| The Lab | Our collective workspace — events, projects, and community. |
| Learning partners | Companies and institutions we work with. Preferred in public-facing copy over "sponsors." |

### Words we avoid

| Avoid | Use instead | Why |
|-------|-------------|-----|
| Leverage | Use, explore, try | Corporate jargon. We don't talk like that. |
| Utilize | Use | Same thing, more syllables. |
| Ecosystem | Community, network | Overused, vague, corporate. |
| World-class | (Don't replace — just cut) | We're grounded. Let the work speak. |
| Innovative / innovation | (Describe what actually happened) | Show, don't label. |
| Disrupt / disruption | (Say what you mean specifically) | We're builders, not a pitch deck. |
| Synergy | (Never) | No. |
| Best practices | "What's working," "what we've learned" | We question practices, we don't follow them blindly. |
| Content | "Writing," "article," "post," "experiment write-up" | "Content" is what marketers say. We make specific things. |
| Stakeholders | Name the actual audience | Be specific. "Partners," "builders," "the community." |
| Ship (as primary success metric) | Share, launch, explore | Shipping is fine as a verb, but it's not our measure of success. Learning is. |

### Product and brand names

- **AI Labs SV** — always written this way. Not "AI Labs," "AILABS," "ai labs sv," or "AILabs SV."
- **The Lab** — capitalized when referring to the community's collective work and events.
- No trademark symbols needed at this stage.
- When referencing specific tools (Cursor, Supabase, v0, etc.), mention them naturally — but never position the brand around any single tool. "The tools worth learning right now" is the framing.

### Inclusive language

- Use gender-neutral language (they/them for unknown individuals)
- Avoid ableist language ("crazy," "blind spot," "lame")
- Use "clear" instead of "easy" — what's easy varies by person
- Avoid culturally specific idioms that don't translate between English and Spanish
- "Curious people" not "smart people" or "talented people" — curiosity is the qualifier, not ability

## The Compass: How to Use This Guide

When writing anything for AI Labs SV, run it through these three filters:

1. **Does it sound like us?** Read it out loud. Does it sound bold, direct, and curious — or does it sound like a corporate brochure? If you wouldn't say it at an event, don't write it.

2. **Does it start with a question or lead to one?** Our best copy is question-driven. "Got questions? Good." "What can we build?" "48 hours to answer one question." If the copy doesn't provoke curiosity, rewrite it.

3. **Does it put the community first?** The community is the product. Walter started it, but the community owns it. Copy should spotlight the builders, the experiments, the questions — not the organization.

When in doubt, return to the brand promise: **"You'll leave with better questions than you came with."** If the copy delivers on that feeling, it's working.
