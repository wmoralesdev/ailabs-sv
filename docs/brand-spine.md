# Ai /abs — Brand spine

> Single source of truth for voice, narrative, and copy across `ailabs-web`. Authored once, restated in product copy in different shapes, never re-paragraphed verbatim. This doc supersedes the legacy `ailabs-sv-brand-voice.md` (now in `docs/legacy/`).

## What this doc is for

This is the paragraph the rest of the site borrows from. It powers:

- The hero subheadline on `/`.
- The homepage **Why** moment.
- The founder pull-quote near the founder spread.
- The opening paragraph of every press / partner deck / sponsor email.
- The test for "is this microcopy on-brand?" — if a CTA or section can't trace back to a sentence here, it doesn't belong on the site.

It is **not** the marketing pitch, the elevator pitch, or a feature list. It is the truth-version of what the lab is, in 2–3 sentences, in a voice that sounds like Walter or Daniela talking out loud.

## POV (the canonical paragraph — to be authored by founders)

> Status: frame, not draft. Founders pick a belief shape below (or merge two), then write the final 2–3 sentences in their own voice. Implementation does not ship copy until this section is locked.

### What goes in (max 3 sentences)

1. **A belief.** Something you think is true about AI / building / learning that not everyone would agree with.
2. **Who it's for, named concretely.** Builders in SV, power users, people moving from chat-tab AI into actual repos. Pick the most specific noun.
3. **What you do that defends the belief.** The `because` under it. One short sentence.

### What stays out

- Feature lists (workshops, hackathons, meetups, demo days).
- Audience lists (builders, teams, partners, universities).
- Contrast framing ("not a course," "not a Discord," "not a bootcamp"). See **Brand commitments → Belief-led copy**.
- Abstract mission language ("we believe in the power of community").
- Explanations of your own vocabulary. `criterio` stays unexplained; the word does the work.

### Tests for "good"

- **Journalist test.** Could two sentences of this stand alone in an article without the Ai /abs logo and still mean something?
- **Disagreement test.** Is there a sentence here someone could honestly disagree with? If everyone reading would nod along, it's too generic.
- **Mouth test.** If Walter or Daniela read this out loud at dinner, would it sound like them, or like marketing?
- **Standalone test.** Strip the homepage to just this paragraph. Does the visitor still know who it's for and why it exists?

## Brand commitments (editorial rules)

These rules apply across every page, section, and microcopy on `ailabs-web`.

- **"Lab" is the primary noun.** "Comunidad" is the spine, not the headline. "Cursor Community SV" is honored as the origin name, not the current identity.
- **Criterio is the editorial spine.** Each page defends it from a different angle: homepage **Why**, work-with-us "training that ends in criterio," partners "criterio antes que hype," events "salir menos bloqueado."
- **Builder-first homepage.** Equipo tech, universidades, and aliados are real audiences but they live on their destination pages. The homepage chooses one voice. See **Audience model** below.
- **Cursor Ambassadors + Codex Community Ambassadors is the credibility line.** v0 / Vercel are friends, not titled relationships, and stay in the partner row without "ambassador" framing.
- **"Próximo evento" is always live.** Static event counts ("12 eventos realizados") are dropped wherever they appear. The hero pulls from the same Convex query as `/events`.
- **Spanish-first with EN toggle.** Section labels, eyebrows, pillar names, and offer names are canonical in Spanish. The English version is a translation, not authored independently. Mixed-language section labels (`Community / Collaboration / Enablement`) get translated to Spanish.
- **Belief-led copy, never contrast-led.** No "we are X, not Y" framing anywhere on the site or in this doc. It reads as generic and weakens credibility. Every claim about what the lab is gets stated in the affirmative; visitors infer what the lab isn't from what we choose to talk about, never from us telling them.
- **Builders, not members.** The people in the lab are *builders*. Not "members," "users," "attendees," or "students."

## Voice

The voice stays the same across pages, channels, and languages. Five attributes, all stated in the affirmative.

- **Bold.** Confident, declarative, willing to take a stance. We say things directly and don't hedge. Sounds like: *"48 horas para responder una pregunta: ¿qué podemos construir?"*
- **Direct.** Clear, concise, anti-jargon. We get to the point. No filler. Sounds like: *"Sin slides. Solo experimentos reales."*
- **Curious.** Question-driven. We lead with "why" and "what if." Sounds like: *"¿Tenés una pregunta? Construyamos algo para responderla."*
- **Inclusive.** Open, welcoming, anti-gatekeeping. The only requirement is genuine curiosity. Credentials are welcome but not required. Sounds like: *"Sin barreras. Solo gente curiosa."*
- **Grounded.** Honest about where we are, rooted in El Salvador, specific over grandiose. Sounds like: *"Una comunidad de builders en El Salvador."*

**The rule:** bold and warm always coexist. In energetic moments, dial up bold. In sensitive moments, dial up warm. Neither attribute disappears.

## Tone by channel

| Channel | Tone | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| Website hero / landing | Bold, declarative, manifesto-like | *"Construir con IA no es pedirle código a un chat."* |
| Social media | Punchy, direct, question-led | *"¿Qué construirías con 48 horas y una pregunta?"* |
| Event descriptions | Action-oriented, inviting, specific | *"Trae tu laptop y tus preguntas."* |
| Blog / learning content | Thoughtful, exploratory, honest about failure | *"Tres experimentos que no salieron, y por qué importan más."* |
| Newsletter / email | Personal, conversational, sharing-focused | *"Esto es lo que aprendimos este mes."* |
| Partner / B2B comms | Confident, specific, mission-anchored | *"Somos un lab de 600+ builders. Así trabajamos con equipos."* |

## Tone by situation

| Situation | Tone shift |
|-----------|-----------|
| Event launch | Dial up boldness and energy. Lead with the question the event will explore. |
| Community milestone | Celebratory but grounded. Credit the community, not the organization. |
| Sharing failures or learnings | Honest and curious. Failures are content, not embarrassments. |
| Recruiting partners or sponsors | Confident, never salesy. Lead with the community's value, not metrics. |
| Onboarding new members | Warm and clear. Make the first step obvious and low-pressure. |
| Paid event communication | Transparent about cost and what it enables. The value is self-evident. Never apologize for charging — explain what the ticket makes possible. |
| Incident or mistake | Transparent and direct. Own it, explain it, move forward. |

## Style rules

### Grammar and mechanics

| Rule | Choice | Example |
|------|--------|---------|
| Oxford comma | Yes | *"workshops, hackathons, y demo days"* |
| Headings | Sentence case | *"Donde las preguntas se vuelven proyectos"* |
| Contractions (EN) | Use them | *"we're," "don't," "it's"* |
| Em dashes (EN) | Use sparingly, no spaces | *"starting in El Salvador—growing across LatAm"* |
| Em dashes (ES, user-facing) | Avoid in `luma.md`, `comms.md`, public event copy | Prefer commas, colons, parentheses, hyphens, or middle dot (` · `) |
| Numbers | Numerals for stats, spelled out in prose | *"600+ builders"* but *"una pregunta"* |
| Percent | Symbol | *"40%"* |
| Exclamation marks | Sparingly. Max one per page. | Overuse kills the bold tone. |
| ALL CAPS | Only for section badges / eyebrows | *"EL LAB," "PARTNERS"* — never in body copy |
| Emojis | Avoid in primary copy | OK in social and community channels, never on the website |
| Ellipsis | Avoid | Trails off instead of committing. We commit. |

### Sentence structure

Short sentences. Declarative. When we use fragments, they're intentional — punchy, not lazy.

We favor the pattern **statement, then proof**: *"Creemos que la mejor forma de entender la IA es construyendo. No siguiendo un currículum. No viendo un tutorial."*

We break convention when it serves clarity: *"Sin slides. Solo experimentos reales."* is better than *"En lugar de presentaciones, mostramos experimentos reales."*

### Formatting

- **Bold** for emphasis, used sparingly.
- *Italics* for titles or introducing terms.
- Link text is always descriptive — never "click here," never "más info."
- Lists only when scanning matters (event details, resources). Prose for everything else.

## Terminology

### Words we use

| Term | Why |
|------|-----|
| **Builders** | Our people. Not "members," "users," or "attendees." |
| **Lab** | The collective workspace — events, projects, community. |
| **Criterio** | The editorial spine. Stays unexplained; the word does the work. |
| **Curioso / curiosa** | The disposition we look for. Not a credential. |
| **Construir / construyendo** | Primary verb. Not "leverage," "utilize," or "implement." |
| **Próximo evento** | Always live, never static. |
| **Aliado / aliados de aprendizaje** | Companies and institutions we work with. Preferred over "sponsors" in public-facing copy. |

### Words we avoid

| Avoid | Use instead | Why |
|-------|-------------|-----|
| Leverage | Use, explore, try | Corporate jargon. |
| Utilize | Use | Same thing, more syllables. |
| Ecosystem (in marketing copy) | Comunidad, red | Overused, vague. We can use it about the broader SV tech scene, never about ourselves. |
| World-class | (Cut it) | We're grounded. Let the work speak. |
| Innovative / innovation | (Describe what actually happened) | Show, don't label. |
| Disrupt / disruption | (Say what you mean specifically) | We're builders, not a pitch deck. |
| Synergy | (Never) | No. |
| Best practices | "Lo que está funcionando," "lo que aprendimos" | We question practices, not follow them blindly. |
| Content | "Escrito," "post," "experimento" | "Content" is what marketers say. |
| Stakeholders | Name the actual audience | Be specific: "partners," "builders," "la comunidad." |
| Members | Builders | See **Brand commitments → Builders, not members**. |

### Product and brand naming

- **Ai /abs** is the canonical brand name. The slash-styling is the lockup; in plain text, "Ai Labs" is acceptable. Avoid "AILABS," "ai labs sv," "AILabs SV."
- **Ai /abs SV** is acceptable when geography needs to be explicit (international audiences, partner decks). Default is just **Ai /abs**.
- **The Lab / El Lab** is capitalized when referring to the collective work and events.
- When referencing tools (Cursor, v0, Codex, Supabase, etc.), mention them naturally. Never position the brand around any single tool. We are the lab; tools are how we work.

### Inclusive language

- Gender-neutral by default in Spanish (lenguaje neutro o paréntesis: *builders curiosos / curiosas*; in formal copy, prefer plural inclusive forms).
- Avoid ableist metaphors ("crazy," "blind spot," "lame").
- Use "claro" instead of "fácil" — what's easy varies by person.
- Avoid culturally specific idioms that don't translate between English and Spanish.

## Bilingual strategy

`ailabs-web` is Spanish-first with an English toggle (mirror, not separate authoring).

### Principles

- **Spanish is canonical.** All section labels, eyebrows, pillar names, offer names, and CTAs are authored in Spanish first.
- **English is the translation.** Adapt, don't translate literally. The tone should feel native in both languages.
- **No mixed-language UI.** Section labels like *Community / Collaboration / Enablement* on a Spanish page get translated.
- **Spanglish is acceptable inside body copy** when it reads naturally for the audience: Cursor, AI SDK, stack, deploy, build, repo, agente, prompt. Editorial labels stay Spanish.

## The compass: how to use this guide

When writing anything for `ailabs-web`, run it through three filters:

1. **Does it sound like us?** Read it out loud. Bold, direct, curious — or corporate brochure? If you wouldn't say it at an event, don't write it.
2. **Does it trace back to the POV?** Pick the sentence in the POV paragraph that this microcopy connects to. If you can't, the microcopy is off-brand.
3. **Does it put builders first?** Builders are the lab. Walter and Daniela started it; the lab belongs to the people in it. Copy spotlights the builders, the experiments, the questions — not the organization.

When in doubt, return to the POV paragraph. If the copy is consistent with one of its sentences, it's working.
